About Me

Hi, I'm Aldi. A crypto enthusiast and self-learner from Indonesia.

I don't have a formal tech degree. I'm just learning things step by step. But there's one big issue I always face when trying to learn something new, especially programming or anything with code. I prefer learning by building, but once I finish something, I often forget how I even built it.

Like with this website... I built it while learning, and now that it's done, I already forgot which tags I used, how I structured the code, and so on. I usually just follow YouTube tutorials and try things out. Do you have any tips so I don't keep forgetting everything I learn? Should I rebuild the site over and over again until it sticks?

Feel free to send me a message with your advice. I don't mind if you roast me, curse, or whatever, as long as it comes with something helpful.

Thanks for stopping by!

Purpose

I created this site to help anyone who wants to understand crypto, blockchain, and web3 in a simpler and more beginner-friendly way.

This site isn't just about learning. There's also a News section to keep up with the latest updates in crypto and the economy, and a Blog where I share my thoughts, experiences, and learning journey.

I believe knowledge, especially in future tech like this, should be free and accessible to everyone. You don't need a degree, connections, or a lot of money to start learning.

Support My Vision (1/2)

When I first discovered that AI was real and usable, I imagined something way beyond just smart chatbots. For me, intelligence isn't just about knowing a lot of stuff. It's about learning, understanding, adapting, and creating. Real intelligence is a process, not just an outcome.

But after using tools like ChatGPT, Bard (now Gemini), Claude, and others, I felt a bit disappointed. They're smart, sure, but not truly intelligent. They can't grow or think independently. They don't remember past experiences across sessions or evolve like humans. Their "intelligence" feels more like a fancy metaphor than the real thing.

As I kept learning more about AI, I found that some projects are actually trying to build what I had in mind. But I don't want to just sit and wait. I want to learn and build it myself.
